Thoughts on personal growth（20200611）

Posted on 2020-06-11 Edited on 2020-06-13 In Career Disqus:
Symbols count in article: 2.5k Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

Chinese Version： 关于个人成长的思考（20200611）-谷歌机翻版

Introduction

Recently I had 1-on-1 catch ups with my manager and mentors, here are some thoughts worth noting down for my record.

Influence and Persuasion

To build one’s influence successfully means your bosses and peers buy in your ideas at critical decision points. I have observed that some people step back when decisions need to be made, but once decisions are made, they groan that the decision is poor, and he is not happy with it. Hypothetically, Let’s assume a scenario where several proposals are for the boss to pick. If he chooses one proposal touted by someone who is very good at persuasion, although it is the worst proposal in your opinion, you still have to bear with the consequences of decision made. Because you don’t try to step in.

Therefore, lean in when with a valid perspective, but try to integrate presentation, persuasion, selling, and negotiation skills to convince others. Gradually, the positive image and branding will build up. These skills are not acquired instantly, but with this mindset in head, it should gradually improve over time with consistent practice.

Ask WHY, then WHAT

I know the WHY and WHAT are already cliche, but just want to add some takeaways based on my personal experience. For example, because I work in a big team, and it is common for manager to split workloads among team members. when I was assigned with a jira, normally it is to develop a feature in one service, recently I tend to limit myself to assigned work, without wanting to learn about what others are doing. But the key to grow and be able to take on bigger responsibility in a team lies in your capability to understand the domain knowledge and business flows end to end. Limiting yourself to a narrow spotlight will prohibit learning and understanding the big picture, and detrimental to personal growth.

Therefore,

  • When assigned with tasks, always actively ask why it has to be done in certain way, approach stakeholders to understand it from multiple perspectives, including Business Analyst(BA), Program Manager, Client. Dive into details on WHAT, WHEN and HOW, to learn about the decisions made, and have an opinion to give feedback on those decisions.
  • Secondly, talk with teammates to understand what they are working on to learn about the whole picture.
  • Thirdly, when making product fix, don’t assume the existing code will work with your fix perfectly well, even though you fix is one line of code. Always carefully scrutinize the code and check with BA on the expected behavior.

Make yourself visible

Visibility impacts your future opportunities, put it in a blunt but simple way. In a department with 100+ employees, become the go-to person of others by helping them and actively participating in events. Grab the opportunity to present your expertise to others, and let them especially management teams recognize your potential.

Let’s reflect, summarize, and grow together :)
